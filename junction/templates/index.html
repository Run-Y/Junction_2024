CO2 Emissions

CO2 emissions are a primary contributor to global warming, mainly resulting from the burning of fossil fuels. Monitoring CO2 levels is essential to understanding and mitigating climate change.

×

Sea Level Rise

Sea level rise is caused by melting glaciers and thermal expansion of seawater due to global warming. This poses a significant threat to coastal regions and island nations.

×

Glacier Ice Mass Loss

Glacier ice mass loss is a clear indicator of climate change, driven by rising global temperatures. The melting of glaciers contributes to sea level rise and affects freshwater supplies.

×

Space Data & Climate Change

Satellite data plays a critical role in understanding climate change. Observing Earth from space enables scientists to monitor global temperature changes, CO2 emissions, ice mass loss, and sea level rise with unprecedented accuracy. These insights allow us to track long-term climate trends, make predictions, and evaluate the effectiveness of climate action initiatives.

Recommendations for addressing climate change

Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges we face today. Global warming, rising sea levels, and an increase in extreme weather events pose threats to human life and ecosystems. Here are some action suggestions to support the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal (SDG 13: Climate Action):

  • Reduce greenhouse gas emissions: Promote the use of clean energy such as solar and wind power, and gradually reduce dependence on fossil fuels.
  • Protect forests and vegetation: Trees are nature's carbon absorbers, and protecting forests and increasing vegetation can help absorb carbon dioxide.
  • Promote green transportation: Reduce vehicle use and encourage public transportation, cycling, and walking as eco-friendly alternatives.
  • Reduce waste: Cut down on energy and water waste in daily life, and improve resource efficiency.
  • Education and advocacy: Strengthen climate change education to raise awareness of its impacts and the importance of taking action.

Each of us can contribute to protecting the environment and promoting sustainable development.
